Introduction

NSE (National Stock Exchange) is an institution of national importance with international stature. We are a trusted market infrastructure institution with high standards of corporate governance.


A homegrown brand with a global vision, NSE is counted as one of the world's largest exchanges and a catalyst for driving India's economic growth. NSE was the first exchange in India to implement electronic or screen-based trading which began its operations in 1994; a pioneer in technology which ensures the reliability and performance of its systems through a culture of innovation and investment in technology. NSE operates a market ecosystem to bring in transparency & efficiency.


Our robust state-of-the-art technology platform offers high levels of robustness, safety and resilience for trading and investment opportunities across all asset classes and for all categories of investors. NSE is focused on investor protection and disciplined development of the Indian capital market landscape.

Pre-Open Market Session

The Pre-Open Market session takes place between 9:00 AM to 9:15 AM every trading day. It helps in price discovery before the regular market opens at 9:30 AM.


During this session, orders can be placed, modified, and cancelled. The equilibrium price determined during this session becomes the opening price of the stock for the day.


SessionTimeActivity
Order Entry9:00 AM – 9:08 AMPlace, Modify, Cancel orders
Order Matching9:08 AM – 9:12 AMPrice discovery & order matching
Buffer Period9:12 AM – 9:15 AMTransition to normal market
Normal Market Opens9:30 AMRegular trading begins

Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs)

ETFs are investment funds traded on stock exchanges, similar to individual stocks. They track an index, commodity, bonds, or a basket of assets like an index fund, but trade like a stock on an exchange.


NSE is India's leading exchange for ETF trading, offering a wide range of ETFs covering equity indices, gold, debt, and international indices.

Investing in the Securities Market

The securities market provides an avenue for investors to invest their savings productively. This section helps you understand the basics of investing in the Indian securities market.


How to Start Investing?

To start investing in the securities market, you need to:

  • Open a Demat Account with a registered Depository Participant (DP)
  • Open a Trading Account with a SEBI registered stockbroker
  • Link your Bank Account for fund transfers
  • Complete your KYC (Know Your Customer) verification

On-Boarding Process

The on-boarding process at NSE is designed to be simple, transparent and efficient. Companies wishing to list on NSE must follow a structured process to ensure compliance with all regulatory requirements.

StepActivityTimeline
1Appoint SEBI Registered Merchant BankerBefore filing
2File Draft Red Herring Prospectus (DRHP) with SEBI30 days before IPO

Process & FAQs — Becoming an NSE Member

NSE membership allows entities to participate as Trading Members in the Exchange's various market segments. The membership process involves fulfilling eligibility criteria and completing documentation requirements.


Membership Process Steps

StepActivity
1Submit online application via ENIT portal
2Submit required documents and declarations
3Pay application processing fee
4In-person verification and interview

6Pay admission fee and deposit

Online Dispute Resolution (ODR) — SMARTODR

SEBI has mandated an Online Dispute Resolution (ODR) mechanism for the securities market. NSE participates in the SMARTODR platform for resolving investor disputes online.


SMARTODR is a convenient, cost-effective, and time-bound platform for resolving disputes between investors and market intermediaries without visiting physical arbitration centers.
